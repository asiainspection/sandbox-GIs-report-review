## 2. Production status

Follow QIMA Standard rules.

| ID | Situation | Instruction |
|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | Goods at another location | If goods belonging to the supplier are at another location no more than 5–15 minutes' walk away, proceed with the inspection. Reflect the location in the report and notify the back office. A 5–15 minute walk is acceptable if the inspection address changes; contact the back office for confirmation. For new suppliers in China, ask them by e-mail to reconfirm the inspection address in advance. |
| 2.2 | Products not ready for inspection | Check the available product and semi-finished product, and ask the factory to provide packing material if available. Mark the inspection as FAILED. If there is no product (or semi-finished product) available at the factory, issue a MISSED REPORT. |
| 2.3 | When to contact the quality team | Contact the QIMA quality team if the inspection takes place in a warehouse that does not belong to the factory or the supplier. |
| 2.4 | Inspector arrives and goods are not ready | Wait until the factory can provide at least part of the goods for inspection, and notify the back office and SIC. Wait until 2:00 PM local time. If goods are still not ready by then, issue a missing inspection report and notify the client. |

## 9. Report writing rules

| ID | Checkpoint | Instructions |
|---|---|---|
| 9.1 | General instruction | Verify all on-site findings are fully aligned with the specification file (product, plug type, packaging). All items must strictly match the booking specification — no assumptions. See Section 12 (FAQ / YTD claims). |
| 9.2 | Vendor and Factory name | Reflect vendor and factory info in the report. Factory name, address, and representative must always be in **English**, even if the factory confirms them in Chinese. |
| 9.3 | General cosmetics | Each SKU must include: product measurement info & photos; carton box size, weight & photos (include the carton photo even when there is no SKU marking on the carton); packing photos; shipping mark photos. If several SKUs are inspected, include one clear picture per design/color (e.g. 2 SKUs, 3 designs + 5 designs = 3 photos + 5 photos). Always check photos are named/tagged by SKU. Product quantity must be consistent throughout the report. Always add barcode scanning results per SKU, with photos of the label and the scan result. Compare product specification against client documentation. Use the TAG feature in QIMAone to organize photos with captions per SKU (see tutorial: https://www.loom.com/share/8ba5648b963740f79fffcc754f524edd). |
| 9.4 | Example photos | Take one product picture per SKU; if an SKU has multiple colors, take a separate picture per color. All inspected SKUs should appear together in one photo on the first page (e.g. 20 SKUs inspected → 1 photo showing all 20). Always include a front view of the product on the main page. |
| 9.5 | Report remarks | Each remark should reference the relevant page number for traceability. Use correct grammar. |
| 9.6 | Color boxes | Take one picture per surface (e.g. a square box with 6 surfaces = 6 pictures). Enlarge the client's specification artwork for detailed comparison against the gift box. |

   - Number of days delayed between the original and actual inspection date (mandatory even for a missing report — "N/A" is not accepted).
   - Factory details.
   - Vendor details.
   - Picture of the factory's production line (mandatory).

## 12. FAQ & Validated workload — Previous mistakes and correct answers (YTD Claims)

The following real claims were raised by Cemaco on past reports. Each is converted into a rule inspectors must actively check going forward, with the mistake ("Error") and what should have happened instead ("Correct").

### 12.1 Wrong hangtag mixed between two products

- **What happened (Error):** Two different products' hangtags were mixed up — Product 1 was inspected wearing Product 2's hangtag. The report result showed PASS despite the mismatch. *(cf. Image 1 / C1)*
- **Correct approach:** The hangtag attached to each product must be checked against that exact product's SKU and specification file. Any mismatch is a fail and must be reported, with a close-up photo showing the hangtag text/SKU next to the product.

### 12.2 Barcode / hangtag number mismatch not reported

- **What happened (Error):** The barcode number on the product and the number on the hangtag did not match the packing file, and this discrepancy was not reported. *(cf. Image 1 / C2)*
- **Correct approach:** The barcode on the product must be verified against both the hangtag and the packing file. Any deviation between the three must be flagged and reported, regardless of severity.

### 12.3 Warning sticker not securely pasted

- **What happened (Error):** The warning sticker on the inner/unit packaging was loose, with lifting edges, and this was not reported. *(cf. Image 2 / C3)*
- **Correct approach:** The warning sticker must be firmly adhered with no lifting edges, positioned per the product manual, and its dimensions verified against specification. Any looseness must be flagged.

### 12.4 Carton quantity marking mismatch not reported

- **What happened (Error):** For SKU 1024200, the carton was marked "400" while the order file specified 136 pieces. The discrepancy was not reported; the inspector's note stated "if no mention, we consider there is no problem." *(cf. Image 2 / C4, Image 9)*
- **Correct approach:** The carton marking (Ctn. No. / quantity) must match the approved order quantity exactly. Absence of a client remark does NOT mean a discrepancy is acceptable — any mismatch between the carton marking and the order file must always be flagged and reported.

### 12.5 Wrong plug type missed (SKU 1206624)

- **What happened (Error):** The product was fitted with an EU-type plug, while the booking specification required a UL-certified plug. This major issue was missed, and the report summary on page 1 showed "Pass" with no mention of the issue. *(cf. Image 3 / C5, Image 10)*
- **Correct approach:** Plug type and certification marks must be verified against the booking specification for every E&E product. Any mismatch is a major finding and must be reflected both in the detailed checkpoint and in the first-page summary/result — never left out of the top-level result.

### 12.6 Non-market energy label not reported

- **What happened (Error):** A yellow "ENERGYGUIDE" sticker was visible on the product, even though this label is not required for the client's market. The large, visually unacceptable label — which does not appear in the approved artwork — was not reported. *(cf. Image 3 / C6, Image 11)*
- **Correct approach:** Label requirements must be checked against the booking specification for the destination market before the inspection. Any label present that is not required (or absent when required) must be flagged, including when it does not match the approved artwork or product information.

### 12.7 Incorrect LED count not reported

- **What happened (Error):** The product packaging stated "6 PCS LED LIGHT," while the booking specification clearly required "10PCS LED LIGHT." This discrepancy was not mentioned in the inspection report. *(cf. Image 4 / C7, Image 12)*
- **Correct approach:** Any printed spec on the product or its packaging (component counts, feature counts, etc.) must be cross-checked word-for-word against the booking specification. Any deviation, however small it may seem, must be flagged.

### 12.8 Color variation across the same item not detected (post-shipment claim, R-Cloud-2321196)

- **What happened (Error):** The same bedspread series ("Ivanna," light blue) showed a large color variation on the shelf — some units appeared white, others green-blue. The report only tested color shading for the light pink item, not the blue item, so the issue was invisible in the report.
- **Correct approach:** Color shade must be checked for **all** inspected items and all colors selected for inspection — not just one representative color per SKU or series (see Section 7, "Color shade").

### 12.9 Insert card color not compared to Pantone (post-shipment claim, R-Cloud-2321196)

- **What happened (Error):** The factory produced the insert card in at least 3 different background colors. The artwork specified a Pantone number for the insert card, but the inspector did not compare the actual card color against the Pantone reference, nor place the packaging together to check for variation.
- **Correct approach:** The Pantone reference on the client's artwork must always be compared against the actual insert card / color box, with a photo of the comparison. If TCX/TPG Pantone references are unavailable, use the STD Pantone (see Section 7, "Pantone").
